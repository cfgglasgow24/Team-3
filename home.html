For over 30 years Deafblind Scotland has been serving the Deafblind community, sustaining deafblind people's personal and professional support networks and influencing statutory services and policy. The work we do raises the awareness of deafblindness, continually highlights the everyday challenges faced by deafblind people and makes a real difference to and for our citizens and their communities across Scotland.

About Us

Our work increases awareness of deaf blindness, consistently draws attention to the difficulties that deafblind individuals encounter on a daily basis, and actually affects change by bringing our charity's concerns to the attention of the Scottish Parliament.

Vision

"A society in which deafblind people have the permanent support and recognition necessary to be equal citizens."

Our Aims

  • To help deafblind people in Scotland live as rightful members of their own communities
  • To encourage and support contact between deafblind poeple and sighted hearing people
  • To liaise with health and social servce providers to make appropriate assistance available to deafblind people in Scotland
  • To work in partnership with statutort and other agencies to improve the quality of life of deafblind people
  • To raise awareness of both the needs and potential of deafblind people
  • To encourage the provision of facilities, services and support for deafblind people

    • Information About Mental Health

    It is true that mental health issues can impact anyone, and there are various factors that can contribute to this. Environmental factors such as trauma, poverty, social isolation, and difficult childhood experiences can all play a significant role. Additionally, disability is often a key factor in mental health challenges. It is important to be aware of these factors and to seek help when needed in order to maintain good mental health. Our organisation contributes to increasing mental health awareness for individuals who are deafblind. specifically by following our future road map of networking between deafblind people and people who are learning tactile.
